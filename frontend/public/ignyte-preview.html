The idea

One team. One theme. A game by the end.

You walk in with your team, the theme drops, and the clock starts. What comes out the other side is a playable game with your name on it.

Pick a role. Make it yours.

Nine roles to pick from. Find the one that fits and own it.

Build something real.

No mockups, no slides. A playable thing, start to finish, on a screen by day two.

Show it. Win it.

Day two ends on the big screen. Six awards are on the line, voted by the room.

Why Pyreloch runs this

Born in the valley. Built for it.

Pyreloch is a video game company from Kashmir. We make games, and we grow the people who make them.

The valley is full of storytellers, artists and coders. Most of them never get the chance to turn that into a career. Pyreloch exists to change that. We train. We hire. We build a path from a kid who likes drawing to a professional who ships games.

Ignyte is where a lot of them start. You show up, you find your role, you build something with your hands. If it sticks, there's a road from here into the industry. If it doesn't, you still walked away with a game you made.

The two days

Day one you start. Day two you ship.

No filler, no warm-up rounds. The clock starts and you're building.

01

Day One

It opens with an interactive lecture on where computer science can actually take you — the paths beyond just "coding." Then the theme drops. You lock roles with your team, sketch a concept, and start building. By sundown something's moving on a screen.

  • Morning · Interactive talk: the roads through CS
  • Midday · Theme revealed at kickoff
  • Afternoon · Lock your roles and scope
  • Nightfall · First playable on a screen
02

Day Two

You polish. You break things and fix them. You cut whatever you can't finish. Then you put your game on the big screen, play everyone else's, and find out who takes the awards home.

  • Morning · Polish, test, cut
  • Afternoon · Showcase on the big screen
  • Evening · Awards, voted by the room

The theme stays sealed until kickoff. You'll find out the same moment everyone else does.
